About

Who I Am

I'm an embedded firmware engineer with 5+ years of experience building real-time control systems, bootloaders, and networked embedded devices. I've taken products from board bring-up through production across industrial controls, mobile platforms, and automotive systems.

I'm most comfortable working close to the hardware — whether that's debugging protocol issues with a logic analyzer or architecting firmware that'll run reliably for years. Currently focused on ARM Cortex-M platforms, industrial communication protocols, and CI/CD pipelines that actually catch bugs before they ship.

I hold an M.S. in Electrical & Computer Engineering (Embedded Systems) from San Diego State University (GPA 3.67) and have shipped firmware at Q-PAC Systems, Qualcomm, and DURA Automotive.
